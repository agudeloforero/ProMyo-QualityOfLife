## ğŸ§  Background

The development of upper-limb prostheses has advanced significantly through **additive manufacturing** and **open-source design initiatives**, such as **e-NABLE**, which foster global collaboration to provide accessible prosthetic solutions (Ten Kate et al., 2017).  
However, many prosthetic devices are still **economically inaccessible** or **difficult to adapt** to individual anatomical needs.  

By integrating **reverse engineering**, **3D scanning**, and **bio-signal control**, ProMyo seeks to demonstrate a viable, replicable, and sustainable model for prosthetic design in Latin America.
